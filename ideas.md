# FTU Trips Website - Design Brainstorm

## Context
- Bilingual website (EN/AR) for FTU Adventure trips under Beyond Destination
- Two trips: Scotland and South Africa
- Tabbed content: Itinerary, Accommodation, Included/Excluded/T&C, Recommendations
- Brand colors: White and Purple (Beyond Destination), Adventure aesthetic (FTU)
- Clean, modern, soft interface with warm visuals and emotional storytelling

## Idea 1: "Expedition Journal" — Editorial Travel Magazine Style

**Design Movement:** New Editorial / Travel Magazine Aesthetic
**Core Principles:**
1. Large-scale photography as the primary storytelling medium
2. Asymmetric editorial layouts with overlapping elements
3. Sophisticated typography hierarchy with serif headlines and clean body text
4. Muted earth tones punctuated by the brand purple

**Color Philosophy:** A warm palette of ivory (#FDFAF6), deep charcoal (#1A1A2E), muted sage (#8B9A7B), and Beyond Destination purple (#6B3FA0) as the accent. The warmth evokes travel nostalgia while purple signals premium curation.

**Layout Paradigm:** Magazine-spread layout with full-bleed hero images, offset text blocks, and staggered card grids. Content flows like turning pages of a luxury travel magazine. Each trip card uses a dramatic vertical image with overlaid text.

**Signature Elements:**
1. "Torn paper" edge dividers between sections
2. Compass rose / map pin motifs as decorative accents
3. Handwritten-style day numbers in the itinerary

**Interaction Philosophy:** Smooth page-like transitions. Tabs slide in like magazine pages. Hover reveals additional photography. Scroll-triggered parallax on hero images.

**Animation:** Fade-up on scroll for content blocks, subtle parallax on hero images, smooth tab transitions with a slide effect, image zoom on hover.

**Typography System:** Playfair Display for English headlines, Tajawal for Arabic text, DM Sans for English body. Bold headlines at 48-64px, body at 16-18px.

## Idea 2: "Terrain Explorer" — Topographic Adventure Style

**Design Movement:** Neo-Brutalist meets Outdoor Adventure
**Core Principles:**
1. Bold, unapologetic typography that commands attention
2. Topographic map patterns and contour lines as texture
3. High contrast between dark backgrounds and vibrant accent colors
4. Modular card system with strong borders and shadows

**Color Philosophy:** Deep navy (#0F1729) as the primary dark, warm sand (#E8D5B7), forest green (#2D5016), and Beyond Destination purple (#7B4FBF). The dark base creates drama while earthy accents ground the adventure theme.

**Layout Paradigm:** Stacked full-width sections with alternating dark/light backgrounds. Trip cards use a "terrain card" metaphor with elevation-style layering. Tab content uses a sidebar navigation on desktop, bottom tabs on mobile.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Topographic contour line patterns as section backgrounds
2. Elevation badges showing trip difficulty/altitude
3. Dotted route lines connecting day-to-day itinerary items

**Interaction Philosophy:** Snappy, tactile interactions. Tabs click with a satisfying state change. Cards have depth on hover. Scroll progress indicator styled as a trail marker.

**Animation:** Spring-based tab transitions, card lift on hover with shadow deepening, route line drawing animation for itinerary, staggered card entrance.

**Typography System:** Space Grotesk for English headlines, IBM Plex Sans Arabic for Arabic, Space Grotesk for body. Headlines at 40-56px with tight letter-spacing, body at 15-17px.

## Idea 3: "Wanderlust Folio" — Warm Luxury Travel Portfolio

**Design Movement:** Soft Luxury / Warm Minimalism
**Core Principles:**
1. Generous whitespace with warm ivory backgrounds
2. Photography-forward with soft rounded frames and subtle shadows
3. Elegant but approachable typography combining serif and sans-serif
4. Gentle color transitions and soft gradients

**Color Philosophy:** Warm ivory (#FAF8F5) as the canvas, rich espresso (#2C1810) for text, warm terracotta (#C4704B) for accents, and Beyond Destination purple (#7B52B4) for CTAs and branding. The palette feels like a luxury travel brochure — warm, inviting, premium.

**Layout Paradigm:** Flowing single-column layout with generous margins. Trip cards use a "postcard" metaphor with rounded corners and soft shadows. Content sections alternate between full-width imagery and contained text blocks. Tabs use pill-shaped selectors with smooth transitions.

**Signature Elements:**
1. Rounded image frames with soft drop shadows (like polaroid photos)
2. Subtle grain texture overlay on backgrounds for warmth
3. Elegant divider lines with small diamond or dot ornaments

**Interaction Philosophy:** Everything feels smooth and intentional. Tabs morph with a sliding indicator. Images have a gentle scale on hover. Scroll reveals content with a soft fade-up. The language toggle is a beautifully designed pill switch.

**Animation:** Smooth 300ms ease transitions for tabs, gentle fade-up-and-in for scroll reveals, soft scale (1.02) on image hover, language toggle with a satisfying slide animation.

**Typography System:** Cormorant Garamond for English headlines (elegant serif), Tajawal for Arabic text (clean, rounded), Outfit for English body (modern sans-serif). Headlines at 36-52px, body at 16px with 1.7 line-height.

## Selected Approach: Idea 3 — "Wanderlust Folio" (Warm Luxury Travel Portfolio)

This approach best aligns with Beyond Destination's brand identity — clean, modern, soft with warm visuals. The warm ivory canvas, photography-forward design, and elegant typography create a premium travel experience that feels both luxurious and approachable. The soft rounded elements and generous whitespace match the user's preference for emotional storytelling and visual clarity.
